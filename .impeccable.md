## Design Context

### Users
Minecraft builders, creators, and players using fast browser-based utilities to solve practical in-game problems. They are usually dropping in with a specific job to do, so the UI should feel immediate, readable, and low-friction.

### Brand Personality
Practical, atmospheric, handcrafted. The tools should feel confident and polished, with a little playful Minecraft energy, rather than like generic dashboard software.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark-forward utility interfaces with layered, game-adjacent backgrounds, soft environmental glow, and subtle material depth. `Mob Dub` and `Potions Plus` are the reference direction for the product family. Light mode can stay available where it already exists, but it should feel airy and sunlit rather than sterile blue-gray. Avoid obvious wallpaper grids, flat slate washes, and template-looking SaaS surfaces.

### Design Principles
1. Keep the task fast and clear, then let the atmosphere carry the personality.
2. Make every tool feel like part of one family, even when the accent colors differ.
3. Prefer layered gradients, haze, and vignette over rigid background patterns.
4. Preserve strong readability and contrast against rich backgrounds.
5. Keep decorative treatment supportive rather than distracting.
